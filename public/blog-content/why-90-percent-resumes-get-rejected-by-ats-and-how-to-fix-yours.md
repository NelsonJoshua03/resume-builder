## What Exactly is ATS and Why Should You Care?

ATS stands for **Applicant Tracking System** - software used by 98% of Fortune 500 companies and 75% of Indian companies (including TCS, Infosys, Reliance, and virtually every MNC) to filter resumes before they reach recruiters.

These systems scan your resume for:
- Keywords from the job description
- Proper formatting (not just looks)
- Relevant experience markers
- Skills and certifications
- Education requirements

If your resume doesn't pass this automated gatekeeper, it goes straight to the digital trash bin - no matter how qualified you are.

**The Problem:** Creative templates with columns, graphics, or tables look great to humans but are completely unreadable by ATS. The system sees them as chaotic data and often extracts nothing.

**The Fix:** Stick to clean, single-column layouts with simple formatting. No tables for organizing content.

### 2. âŒ Wrong File Format
**The Problem:** Sending resumes as .JPG, .PNG, or even .DOC (not .DOCX) makes them impossible for ATS to parse correctly.

**The Fix:** Always use **.PDF** (if ATS-friendly) or **.DOCX** format. Our resume builder generates perfectly optimized PDFs that pass every ATS test.

### 3. âŒ Missing Keywords
**The Problem:** ATS scans for specific keywords from the job description. If you don't include them (or use different terminology), you fail automatically.

**The Fix:** Mirror the language from the job description. If they say "Python Development," don't just write "Python Programming."

### 4. âŒ Bad Headers & Footers
**The Problem:** Important information in headers/footers (contact details, certifications) often gets ignored by ATS scanners.

**The Fix:** Keep critical information in the main body of your resume, not in decorative headers.

### 5. âŒ Non-Standard Section Titles
**The Problem:** Creative section titles like "My Journey" or "What I Bring" confuse ATS. It's looking for standard sections: "Work Experience," "Education," "Skills."

**The Fix:** Use industry-standard section headers that every ATS recognizes instantly.

### 6. âŒ Over-Designing with Colors
**The Problem:** Light gray text, colored backgrounds, or gradient designs might look modern but often fail ATS contrast and readability checks.

**The Fix:** Black text on white background. Period. Save creativity for your portfolio.

### 7. âŒ Using Charts & Infographics
**The Problem:** Visual skill meters, progress bars, and infographics are completely invisible to ATS. The system sees empty space where your skills should be.

**The Fix:** List skills in simple bullet points or comma-separated text.
