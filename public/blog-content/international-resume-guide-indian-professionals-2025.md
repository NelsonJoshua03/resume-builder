## The 7 Deadly Sins of Indian Resumes for Global Jobs

### 1. Using Indian CV Format for International Applications
Indian resumes often include:
- Photos (illegal in US/UK screening)
- Personal details (age, marital status)
- Declarations at the bottom
- Over-detailed education (marks from school)

**Fix:** Use the [ATS-friendly US resume format](/ats-friendly-resume-us-jobs-from-india) that removes these elements.

### 2. Ignoring Cultural Keyword Differences
The phrase "managed a team" might be fine in India, but global companies search for:
- "Led cross-functional team"
- "Drove revenue growth"
- "Optimized operational efficiency"

**Fix:** Research job descriptions from your target country and industry.

### 3. Metric System Confusion
Indian: "Increased revenue by 25 lakhs"
International: "Increased revenue by $30,000 (25 lakh INR)"

**Fix:** Always include USD equivalents and use international measurement systems.

### 4. Length Mismatch
- India: 3-4 pages acceptable
- USA: 1 page (freshers), 2 pages max (experienced)
- UK/EU: 2 pages standard

**Fix:** Use our [free resume builder](/free-resume-builder) with country-specific templates.

### 5. Missing Visa/Work Authorization Status
International recruiters need to know immediately if you require sponsorship.

**Fix:** Add a line: "Authorized to work in: [Country] or Requires sponsorship"

### 6. Local References Without Global Context
Indian company: "Worked at Infosys"
Better: "Worked at Infosys (NYSE: INFY, 250,000+ employees, global IT services)"

**Fix:** Add brief context for Indian companies.

### 7. Ignoring Time Zone Differences in Contact Info
If you're in India applying to US companies, include:
- Your availability in their time zone
- Best times to call

## Country-Specific Resume Requirements

### United States & Canada
- **Format:** Reverse chronological
- **Length:** 1 page (0-5 years), 2 pages (5+ years)
- **Keywords:** "results-driven," "ROI," "KPI," "scalable solutions"
- **Avoid:** Photo, age, marital status
- **Essential:** LinkedIn profile URL

### United Kingdom & EU
- **Format:** Skills-based or hybrid
- **Length:** 2 pages standard
- **Keywords:** "compliance," "GDPR," "sustainable," "multilingual"
- **Include:** Language proficiency with CEFR levels (B2, C1, etc.)
- **Note:** Some EU countries expect photo

### Australia & New Zealand
- **Format:** Detailed, comprehensive
- **Length:** 3-4 pages acceptable
- **Keywords:** "ANZAC," "APAC experience," "remote collaboration"
- **Include:** Specific visa information

### Singapore & Middle East
- **Format:** Conservative, professional
- **Length:** 2-3 pages
- **Keywords:** "cross-cultural," "emerging markets," "multinational"
- **Include:** Photo (professional), languages

## Visa & Sponsorship: What to Include

### For H-1B (USA) Applicants
Add section: "US Employment Authorization"
- H-1B cap-exempt status
- Previous US employment
- Specialty occupation alignment

### For UK Skilled Worker Visa
Include:
- SOC code matching
- Points calculation summary
- English language proof

### For Canada Express Entry
Mention:
- CRS score (if known)
- Language test scores (IELTS/CELPIP)
- ECA reference number
